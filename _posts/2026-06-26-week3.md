This week's focus shifted from adding new policies to fixing bugs in what already existed, and starting to grapple with a harder design question: when should the system actually stop and ask a human, versus just handling something on its own?

**Fixed this week:** the run time was being computed wrong in a few cases, message coloring for "weird" or off-topic messages wasn't rendering correctly, and a persona I'd added — Alexander, a new participant who kept angling for soccer instead of basketball — needed real handling instead of being treated like a normal RSVP.

Alexander turned out to be a good stress test. He'd reply "in" but then pitch a different sport, different location, and different vibe than what was actually being organized. That raised a real question I don't have a clean answer to yet: should the system flag it the *first* time someone floats an alternative like this, even before we know if they're serious about it, or only once it's clear they're not budging? Right now it leans toward flagging early — understanding the *first* message, not waiting for the coordinator's final read on the situation — but I'm not fully sold this is right in every case.

By 6/23, I could see this working end-to-end in a real run: the dashboard flagged Alexander's off-topic suggestion the moment it appeared, tagged it with a reason ("suggested soccer"), and let me resolve it with a quick reply rather than letting the coordinator silently reinterpret what was going on.

**Working on:** drafting good messages is turning out to be harder than writing the logic that decides *when* to send one — I'm spending more time now on message quality than on the underlying flagging system. Also still working through how the system should behave when it needs to loop the coordinator in on something novel: right now that's fairly manual, and I want it to be a first-class part of the flow rather than a workaround.

**Next steps:** I want a clearer visual distinction in the dashboard between messages the system drafted/sent on its own versus messages a person is actively deciding on — possibly a dedicated column for the agent itself, separate from the per-participant columns that exist now.
